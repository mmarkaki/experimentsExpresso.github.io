Same Speaker Resynthesis

In this section, we ask to reconstruct expressive Samples from Expresso dataset using the same speaker voice as the input. This tests whether the expressive styles can be generalized to other samples from the same speaker.

Resynthesis results from baseline systems using discrete units from Encodec-, HuBERT- (both samples from https://speechbot.github.io/expresso/) and FACodec-based models are presented. Encodec-based models have been directly trained with a compression objective to encode and decode speech using 8 codebooks (Encodec 8). The HuBERT-based models use a pretrained HuBERT encoder: V1, the best model, is trained on a mixture of read and spontaneous corpora. The HuBERT embeddings (layer 12 for V1) are then followed by kmeans clustering (k=2000) on Expresso (E) dataset (https://github.com/facebookresearch/textlesslib/tree/main/examples/expresso/dataset). Finally, a HifiGAN vocoder trained on Expresso, VCTK and LJ speech converts the discrete units back to speech. The vocoder is conditioned on both speaker and expression (S+E) (best model V1/2000_E/S+E).

FACOdec is just a Codec which tokenizes audio to RVQ indexs which can be used in TTS models, like vall-e, natural speech3 or VoiceCraft and so on. FACodec-based models use code from https://github.com/lifeiteng/naturalspeech3_facodec

Representative samples from the most difficult expressions in the dev set of the Expresso dataset are presented here, along with the resynthesized outputs of our encoder/decoder models, in order to illustrate the challenges of the Expresso dataset.

Samples are divided into Read and Conversation sections; the singing example illustrate the fact that HuBERT units do not encode pitch, and many extreme pitch excursions (as in child or animal), result in voiceless renderings. In contrast, Encodec and FACodec units encode well all expressions.

Out-of-domain Speaker Resynthesis - LJ voice transfer

In this section, the vocoder is asked to reconstruct an expressive sample from Expresso, but using a speaker voice that is outside of the expresso dataset (LJ voice), and was therefore never recorded with this particular expression.

This tests whether the Expresso styles can be generalized outside of the small set of 4 speakers to standard TTS voices. Note that the LJ voice can be made to laugh and whisper and to a certain extent change it's tone of voice according to the required expressive style.

Whereas HuBERT units are speaker independent and can be used to resynthetize a message in a target voice that is different from the input voice, Encodec units represent their inputs hollistically with all their acoustic characteristics including voice and therefore cannot perform voice transfer. Thus only results with Hubert models are compared to FACOdec which is just a Codec which tokenizes audio to RVQ indexs which can be used in TTS models, like vall-e, natural speech3 or VoiceCraft and so on.
